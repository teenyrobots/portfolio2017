More about me

I'm an news designer, cartoonist, and all-around digital media witch. I design playful communications solutions and help media organizations tell better stories.

In 2012 I cofounded Symbolia, an interactive digital magazine that published long-form journalism told through comics until 2015. From 2015-2016, I was a fellow with the JoLT (Journalism Leadership Transformation) initiative at American University studying the intersection of game design and news media. I teach Digital Comics in the Comics MFA program at California College of the Arts. I've worked with clients like Vox Media, the Online News Association, Bed Bath Beyond, and more. For a full list, have a look at my CV here.

My work has appeared in Columbia Journalism Review, has been written about in FastCo., Poynter, and other outlets, and I have spoken about telling better stories at Game Developer's Conference, VOCER Innovation Fest, on The Verge's Top Shelf, and others (come see me at SXSW17 in March!). I love conversations about journalism, collaboration, and playspaces, and I'm super interested in what different kinds of digital or social interactivity could do to make these things more exciting for audiences.

I have a BFA in Illustration and an MA in Game Design. I'm an expert in Adobe CC (PS, AI, and InD), fluent with HTML, CSS, Unity, and Twine, and familar with Javascript, jQuery, C#, and Construct 2.

I draw, ride bikes, code, and sleep in Chicago (mostly).
